Lesson 17

Grammar - Muset nadvakrát

Slovesa have to a must se do češtiny obě překládají jako muset, a i když je rozdíl mezi nimi jen velmi malý, používají se v odlišných kontextech. Obecně se však dá říct, že obě tato slovesa používají k vyjádření nutnosti nebo povinnosti. Jak mezi nimi úspěšně rozlišovat?

🎯 Zdroj povinnosti

"Must" vyjadřuje spíše vnitřní povinnost nebo nutnost, kterou cítí mluvčí. Je to silný výraz, který se obvykle používá k zdůraznění osobního názoru nebo pocitu povinnosti. "Have to" obvykle vyjadřuje vnější povinnost, která je někým nebo něčím vnucena, například pravidly, zákony nebo okolnostmi.

🧩 I must learn more about cybersecurity to better protect my clients' data. = já osobně to pociťuji jako nutnost

🧩 I must learn the latest programming languages to keep my skills sharp. = vnitřní nutkání

🧩 She must practice her coding regularly to master the new algorithms. = nikdo vnější ji do toho nenutí, sama má zájem

🧩 He must update his personal blog with the latest tech trends to engage his audience. = jeho osobní projekt, nikdo vnější ho do toho nenutí, vlastní nutnost

🧩 I must organize my project files better to improve my productivity. = šéfori to může být jedno, ale mně se bude lépe pracovat

🧩 She must attend the upcoming cybersecurity webinar to enhance her knowledge on the topic. = firma ji do toho nenutí, je to její vlastní zájem

🧩 You have to change your password every 90 days to comply with our IT security policy. = vnější nařízení

🧩 I have to work from 9 to 5 every day. = pracovní doba nucená zaměstnavatelem

🧩 I have to reset my password because I forgot it. = nucení vnějšími okolnostmi

🧩 She has to install the new security patch to protect her computer from viruses. = nucení vnějšími okolnostmi

🧩 We have to back up our files before the system upgrade tomorrow. = nařízení team leadera

🧩 You have to verify your email addresses to activate your accounts. = zákonitost systému

🧩 I have to charge my laptop; the battery is almost dead. = nucení vnější okolností

🧩 Employees have to complete cybersecurity training by the end of the quarter. = firma je do toho nutí

🧩 You have to reboot the server for the changes to take effect. = zákonitost systému

🧩 Users have to agree to the terms and conditions before using the software. = zákonitost systému

⏳ Časovost

"Must" se používá hlavně v přítomném a budoucím čase a nemá vlastní minulý tvar. K vyjádření minulosti se používá "had to". "Have to" se mění podle času ("have to" v přítomném, "had to" v minulém) a osoby ("has to" pro třetí osobu jednotného čísla).

🧩 She has to install the new security patch to protect her computer from viruses.

🧩 The IT specialist has to verify the data backup process every week to ensure data integrity.

🧩 My computer is really slow. It seem I'll have to buy a new one.

🧩 Tomorrow, they will have to present the new software update to the team.

🧩 I had to work overtime yesterday.

🧩 Yesterday, she had to update her computer to fix the security vulnerability.

❓ Otázky

K položení otázek se "must" používá méně často než "have to". Otázky s "have to" jsou běžnější a přirozenější, zvláště v hovorové angličtině.

🧩 What do you have to learn to become a cyber security expert?

🧩 Do I have to restart my computer after installing the new software?

🧩 Do you have to enter a password every time you access the server?

🧩 Does he have to obtain approval for the new firewall settings?

🕵️‍♀️ Must pro logické dedukce

"Must" se v angličtině používá pro vyjádření silného přesvědčení nebo závěru na základě dostupných důkazů nebo logické dedukce. Když použijete "must" v tomto kontextu, dáváte najevo, že jste na základě pozorování nebo známých faktů došli k určitému závěru, i když nemáte přímý důkaz.

🧩 The server is down; there must be a problem with the network.

🧩 She's not responding to her emails; she must be in a meeting.

🧩 The website is loading slowly; there must be a lot of traffic on it right now.

🧩 He always fixes computer issues quickly; he must be very experienced with IT.

🧩 They must be updating the app since it's not available in the store at the moment.

🧩 The printer is out of toner; that must be why it's not printing correctly.

🧩 She understood that complex programming concept right away; she must be very intelligent.

🧩 We received an automated response; the customer service team must be very busy today.

❗ Mustn't ≠ don't have to ❗

Ačkoli mají slovesa must a have to ve svém kladném tvaru stejný význam, jejich zápory se od sebe razantně liší. "Mustn't" vyjadřuje zákaz - tedy něco, co se nesmí dělat. Naproti tomu "don't have to" vyjadřuje, že něco není nutné dělat, protože to není povinné, ale člověk to klidně udělat může, pokud chce.

Mustn't = nesmět (striktní zákaz)

🧩 You mustn't share your password with anyone.

🧩 Employees mustn't use company computers for playing video games.

🧩 You mustn't install new software without first getting approval from IT support.

🧩 We mustn't allow unauthorized users to access the admin panel.

🧩 She mustn't leave her laptop unattended in public spaces to prevent theft.

Don't have to = nemuset (ale klidně můžeš, pokud chceš)

🧩 You don't have to upgrade to the latest software if it doesn't suit your needs.

🧩 They don't have to come to the office on weekends unless there is an urgent issue.

🧩 You don't have to memorize your IP address; the network assigns it automatically.

🧩 We don't have to stay late to finish the project; our deadline was extended.

🧩 She doesn't have to check her emails while on vacation; the team will handle urgent matters.

🔥 Study skills tip 🔥

"Have to" se ve skutečnosti řadí do kategorie frázových modálních sloves, což znamená, že má podobnou funkci jako modální slovesa, ale liší se od nich formou a použitím. Modální slovesa se v angličtině nečasují a nepřijímají infitivní "to" před následujícím plnovýznamových slovesem (must go, can see, may have.) "Have to" se chová více jako běžné sloveso, mění se podle osob ("has to" ve třetí osobě jednotného čísla) a časů ("had to" v minulém čase).

Vocabulary - Slovíčka povolení a omezení

Permission [pəˈmɪʃən]

Approval or consent to do something.

🧩 You need permission to access the server room.

🧩 Without the manager's permission, changes to the software cannot be made.

🧩 He sought permission to upgrade the company's operating system.

Authorisation [ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən]

Official approval or permission.

🧩 Your authorisation to use the software will expire next month.

🧩 The IT department provided authorisation for the system update.

🧩 Without the necessary authorisation, employees cannot access sensitive data.

Consent [kənˈsent]

Voluntary agreement or approval.

🧩 User consent is required before collecting personal data.

🧩 He gave his consent for the software to be installed on his computer.

🧩 The team cannot proceed without the client's consent.

Approval [əˈpruːvəl]

The belief that something or someone is good or acceptable.

🧩 The software update is pending senior management approval.

🧩 The IT proposal will move forward once it receives approval.

🧩 He gained approval to integrate a new security protocol.

Clearance [ˈklɪərəns]

Official permission or approval to proceed.

🧩 Security clearance is required to access this section of the database.

🧩 The IT technician received clearance to repair the company's servers.

🧩 Without proper clearance, files on the network cannot be modified.

Limitation [ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən]

A restriction.

🧩 The software has a limitation on the number of users who can log in simultaneously.

🧩 One limitation of our current system is its storage capacity.

🧩 We need to consider the limitation when transferring large files.

Restriction [rɪˈstrɪkʃən]

A limiting rule or condition.

🧩 There's a restriction on accessing certain files without authorization.

🧩 Due to security restrictions, external drives are not allowed.

🧩 The software license comes with usage restrictions.

Boundary [ˈbaʊndəri]

A limit or edge.

🧩 Setting data boundaries ensures that each department can only access relevant information.

🧩 We need to establish clear boundaries for user permissions.

🧩 The IT project has scope boundaries to keep it on track and within budget.

Hindrance [ˈhɪndrəns]

A thing that provides resistance or obstruction.

🧩 Slow internet connectivity is a hindrance to our daily operations.

🧩 Hardware malfunctions were a major hindrance during the software launch.

🧩 Lack of proper training can be a hindrance to adopting new technologies.

Listening - Python in 100 seconds

Python a high-level interpreted programming language famous for its zen-like code. It's arguably the most popular language in the world because it's easy to learn, yet practical for serious projects. In fact, you're watching this YouTube video in a Python web application right now. It was created by Guido van Rossum and released in 1991 who named it after Monty Python's Flying Circus which is why you'll sometimes find spam and eggs instead of foo and bar in code samples.

It's commonly used to build server-side applications like web apps with the Django framework and is the language of choice for big data analysis and machine learning. Many students choose Python to start learning to code because of its emphasis on readability as outlined by the zen of Python = beautiful is better than ugly while explicit is better than implicit. Python is very simple but avoids the temptation to sprinkle in magic that causes ambiguity.

Its code is often organized into notebooks where individual cells can be executed, then documented in the same place. We're currently at version 3 of the language and you can get started by creating a file that ends in .py or .ipymb to create an interactive notebook. Create a variable by setting a name equal to a value. It's strongly typed which means values won't change in unexpected ways but dynamic so type annotations are not required. The syntax is highly efficient, allowing you to declare multiple variables on a single line and define tuples, lists and dictionaries with a literal syntax.

Semicolons are not required and if you use them, an experience Pythonista will say that your code is not Pythonic. Instead of semicolons, python uses indentation to terminate or determine the scope of a line of code. Define a function with the def keyword then indent the next line usually by four spaces to define the function body. We might then add a for-loop to it and indent that by another four spaces. This eliminates the need for curly braces and semicolons found in many other languages.

Python is a multi-paradigm language. We can apply functional programming patterns with things like anonymous functions using lambda. It also uses objects as an abstraction for data, allowing you to implement object-oriented patterns with things like classes and inheritance. It also has a huge ecosystem of third-party libraries such as deep learning frameworks like Tensorflow and wrappers for many high performance low level packages like open Computer Vision which are most often installed with the Pip package manager.
