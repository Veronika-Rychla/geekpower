Lesson 15

Grammar

Vocabulary - Slovíčka vyjadřující povolení

Permission [pəˈmɪʃən]

Approval or consent to do something.

🧩 You need permission to access the server room.

🧩 Without the manager's permission, changes to the software cannot be made.

🧩 He sought permission to upgrade the company's operating system.

Authorisation [ˌɔːθəraɪˈzeɪʃən]

Official approval or permission.

🧩 Your authorisation to use the software will expire next month.

🧩 The IT department provided authorisation for the system update.

🧩 Without the necessary authorisation, employees cannot access sensitive data.

Consent [kənˈsent]

Voluntary agreement or approval.

🧩 User consent is required before collecting personal data.

🧩 He gave his consent for the software to be installed on his computer.

🧩 The team cannot proceed without the client's consent.

Approval [əˈpruːvəl]

The belief that something or someone is good or acceptable.

🧩 The software update is pending senior management approval.

🧩 The IT proposal will move forward once it receives approval.

🧩 He gained approval to integrate a new security protocol.

Clearance [ˈklɪərəns]

Official permission or approval to proceed.

🧩 Security clearance is required to access this section of the database.

🧩 The IT technician received clearance to repair the company's servers.

🧩 Without proper clearance, files on the network cannot be modified.

Vocabulary - Slovíčka vyjadřující žádosti a návrhy

Request [rɪˈkwɛst]

An act of asking formally or politely for something.

🧩 We received a request to upgrade the office's internet.

🧩 The user sent a request for password recovery.

🧩 I'll submit a request to the IT department for additional software licenses.

Offer [ˈɒfə]

Present something for someone to accept or reject.

🧩 The software company made an offer for a free trial of their new product.

🧩 Our IT team will offer support for any technical issues during the transition.

🧩 The cloud service provider made an attractive offer for data storage solutions.

Suggestion [səˈdʒɛsʃən]

An idea or plan put forward for consideration.

🧩 One suggestion was to enhance our website's user interface.

🧩 The IT specialist made a suggestion to improve system security.

🧩 Based on user feedback, we've received several suggestions for software enhancements.

Proposal [prəˈpəʊzəl]

An official plan or suggestion put forward for consideration or discussion.

🧩 The IT team submitted a proposal for a new data backup system.

🧩 We're reviewing a proposal to migrate our databases to the cloud.

🧩 The proposal outlines the benefits of switching to a new software platform.

Recommendation [ˌrɛkəmɛnˈdeɪʃən]

A gentle proposal or endorsement for someone or something.

🧩 The IT audit resulted in a recommendation to update our servers.

🧩 Based on the system analysis, we have a few recommendations for optimization.

🧩 The cybersecurity expert's recommendation is to enable two-factor authentication.

Vocabulary - Slovíčka vyjadřující omezení

Limitation [ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən]

A restriction or constraint.

🧩 The software has a limitation on the number of users who can log in simultaneously.

🧩 One limitation of our current system is its storage capacity.

🧩 We need to consider the limitation when transferring large files.

Restriction [rɪˈstrɪkʃən]

A limiting rule or condition.

🧩 There's a restriction on accessing certain files without authorization.

🧩 Due to security restrictions, external drives are not allowed.

🧩 The software license comes with usage restrictions.

Boundary [ˈbaʊndəri]

A limit or edge.

🧩 Setting data boundaries ensures that each department can only access relevant information.

🧩 We need to establish clear boundaries for user permissions.

🧩 The IT project has scope boundaries to keep it on track and within budget.

Hindrance [ˈhɪndrəns]

A thing that provides resistance or obstruction.

🧩 Slow internet connectivity is a hindrance to our daily operations.

🧩 Hardware malfunctions were a major hindrance during the software launch.

🧩 Lack of proper training can be a hindrance to adopting new technologies.

Vocabulary - Slovíčka vyjadřující pravděpodobnost

Likelihood [ˈlaɪklihʊd]

The chance or probability of something happening.

🧩 Given the current vulnerabilities, there's a high likelihood of a security breach.

🧩 The likelihood of a software glitch increases if regular updates are skipped.

🧩 The IT team assessed the likelihood of server downtime during the upgrade.

Probability [ˌprɒbəˈbɪlɪti]

The measure of the likelihood that an event will occur.

🧩 The probability of data loss is minimal with our new backup system.

🧩 Given the system's robustness, the probability of a crash is low.

🧩 The IT department calculated the probability of a cyberattack based on recent trends.

Chance [ʧɑːns]

A possibility of something happening, informal.

🧩 There's a good chance that the software patch will resolve the current issues.

🧩 The IT team took no chances and implemented multiple security layers.

🧩 What's the chance of recovering the lost data after the system crash?

Possibility [ˌpɒsɪˈbɪlɪti]

A thing that may happen or be the case.

🧩 We're exploring the possibility of migrating to a cloud-based solution.

🧩 There's a slight possibility that users might experience downtime during the update.

🧩 Given the latest advancements, remote work is now a viable possibility for most IT professionals.

Odds [ɒdz]

The likelihood of a particular outcome, often used in the context of gambling but can be used more broadly.

🧩 The odds of encountering a virus are high if you don't have antivirus software.

🧩 Given the system's age, the odds of hardware failure are increasing.

🧩 With the current security measures, the odds of unauthorized access are minimal.

Certainty [ˈsɜːtənti]

A firm conviction that something is the case; absolute confidence.

🧩 With our new encryption techniques, the certainty of data protection is high.

🧩 Given the test results, there's a certainty that the software is bug-free.

🧩 The IT manager expressed certainty in the team's ability to handle the upcoming challenges.
