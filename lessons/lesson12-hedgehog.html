Lesson 12

Grammar - Budoucnost záměru a schylování

Přítomný průběhový se nejlépe hodí pro vyjádření budoucnosti, která se určitě stane, protože jsme buď na 100% rozhodnuti nebo pro to máme nějakou formu důkazu (nakoupené letenky, schůzku v kalendáři, termín u lékaře). Co když ale chceme vyjádřit náš záměr něco udělat, který ještě není pevným plánem nebo situace, ke kterým se schyluje? K tomu použijeme jednoduchou vazbu be going to + sloveso v základním tvaru.

💪 Budoucnost záměru - rozhodli jsme se něco udělat a opravdu to chceme

Představte si situaci, kdy opravdu hodně chcete navštívit nějakou vysněnou destinaci nebo konferenci a chcete tam jet příští rok. Ještě však nemáte letenky ani odložené peníze a váš záměr je sice silný a už ho v hlavě nosíte nějakou dobu, ale ještě není pevným plánem. I když to opravdu chcete provést a nejspíš to i uděláte, nemůžete ještě říct, že se to stane na 100%. V českém překladu si záměr můžeme představit jako chci něco udělat.

🧩 I'm going to visit Japan next year. = Příští rok chci navštívit Japonsko. Je to naším silným záměrem, ale ještě jsme pro to nic reálně neudělali.

🧩 I'm visiting Japan next year in May. = Příští rok jedu do Japonska. Už mám nakoupené letenky a určitě jedu.

🧩 I'm going to attend FrontKon in October. = V říjnu se chci zúčastnit FrontKonu. Opravdu se toužím zúčastnit, ale ještě nemám lístek, takže je možné, že to nevyjde.

🧩 I'm attending FrontKon in October. = V říjnu jdu na FrontKon. Lístek mám zajištěný.

🧩 We are going to launch the new software next month. = máme v záměru vypustit nový program příští měsíc, ale ještě nemáme pevné datum a je možné, že dojde k odkladu

🧩 I am going to write a script to automate that task. = určitě to mám v úmyslu, ale ještě nemám pevný plán

🧩 They are going to upgrade the server infrastructure in the third quarter. = IT oddělení to chce udělat, ale znáte to, dokud není deadline... 😅

Pomocí going to se můžeme jednoduše ptát druhých, co chtějí udělat. Musí přitom jít o promyšlené záměry a ne spotánní rozhodnutí na místě.

🧩 Are you going to implement that bug fix now? = Máš to v záměru implementovat teď?

🧩 Is she going to attent PyCon? = Chce a je jejím záměrem jít na PyCon? Protože nevíme, jestli už listek má nebo ne, nemůžeme použít přítomný průběhový.

🧩 Are you going to eat that sandwich? = Chceš jíst ten sendvič/máš to v úmyslu? Pokud ne, tak ho chci já! 😎

👀 Pozorujeme, že se k něčemu chyluje

Going to můžeme úspěšně aplikovat i na takové budoucí situace, u kterých pozorujeme, že se k nim pomalu ale jistě schyluje, a máme pro to jasné důkazy. Jde o formu budoucnosti, která není ani záměrem (záměr může mít jen osoba o něco usilující) ani plánem (často to buď nechceme nebo výsledek neovlivníme), ale není ani spontánní.

🧩 It's going to rain. = pozorujeme, jak se obloha zatahuje

🧩 My collagues is going to have a baby. = už to jde na kolegyňce vidět 🤰

🧩 The server is going to crash if we don't address the memory issue. = z diagnostiky jsme vypozorovali, že se k tomu schyluje

🧩 Given the current bugs, the software release is going to be delayed. = máme jasné indikace, které nasvědčují, že se schyluje k odkladu

🧩 He hasn't backed up his data, and he is going to lose everything if his hard drive fails. = Kolega tohle určitě nechce, ale my pozorujeme, že si nezálohuje data, a tak se schyluje k tomu, že o ně přijde.

🔥 Study skills tip 🔥

Pokud vám pochopení schylování se činí potíže, zkuste následující metodu. Rozhlédněte se okolo sebe, projděte se po bytě, vykoukněte z okna. K čemu se pomalu ale jistě schyluje?

🧩 It's going to snow. = vykoukneme z okna a vidíme šedé naducané mraky 🌨

🧩 Judging by the error messages, the system is going to need a reboot. = máme důkaz v hromadě chybových hlášek

🧩 The hard drive is going to malfunction soon; it's already making strange noises. = důkaz slyšíme

🧩 With all these background processes running, the computer is going to overheat. = bojíme se, že k tomu dojde, máme pro to pádné důkazy

🧩 Are we going to have chicken for lunch? = Ucítíme, že partner/partnerka v kuchyni peče maso a voní jako kuře. Nemůžems si být zcela jistí (jinak bychom použili přítomný průběhový), ale nejspíš se k tomu schyluje.

Vocabulary - Strategie a plány

⏰ Plan ⏰

A detailed proposal for achieving or doing something. It is a broader term for a strategy or proposal for achieving a specific goal.

🧩 We need to create a new marketing plan to increase brand awareness.

🧩 We have a plan to clean the house this weekend.

🧩 The teacher has a lesson plan for each subject.

⏰ Strategy ⏰

A plan of action designed to achieve a long-term or overall aim. It often refers to higher-level planning for achieving long-term goals, typically in a competitive environment.

🧩 The company developed a new business strategy to expand into international markets.

🧩 A study strategy can help you learn more effectively.

🧩 We need a good strategy to win this contract.

⏰ Blueprint ⏰

A detailed outline or plan for a building project; also used metaphorically for any detailed plan. It specifically refers to detailed plans for construction but can be used metaphorically for other detailed plans.

🧩 The architect drew up a blueprint for the new house.

🧩 A good blueprint should include all the details of the construction.

🧩 We reviewed the blueprint before starting the project.

⏰ Roadmap ⏰

A plan or guide to show how something is organized or can be achieved. It typically illustrates the stages or steps in developing a product or achieving a goal.

🧩 A clear roadmap can help guide the way forward.

🧩 The team has a roadmap for completing the project.

🧩 Our roadmap includes releasing new features every month.

⏰ Timeline ⏰

A graphic representation of the chronological sequence of past or future events. It visually represents events, milestones, or tasks over time, often in a linear format.

🧩 The project manager created a timeline to visualize the project milestones and deadlines.

🧩 The timeline shows all the important dates in history.

🧩 We created a project timeline to track our progress.

⏰ Checklist ⏰

A list of items required, things to be done, or points to be considered. It is a simple list of items or tasks to be checked off when completed.

🧩 I have a checklist of things to pack for my trip.

🧩 Before starting the engine, the pilot goes through a checklist.

🧩 Make sure to complete everything on your homework checklist.

Listening - AI, machine learning and deep learning

When it comes to AI, machine learning, and deep learning, there is some confusion as to what these words mean and the differences between them. So, in this video, we will explain what AI means and how to differentiate between these three terms.

So, what is artificial intelligence? In the broader sense of the word, artificial intelligence refers to a machine that is made to simulate human consciousness. When the average person thinks of AI, they imagine robots that can see, move, speak, think and possibly even feel the way humans do. For some, the ultimate goal of AI research is to create a sentient, conscious being through programming. Sometimes this is also referred to as artificial general intelligence.

However, in practice, most data scientists are using AI at a smaller, more practical scale to solve real-world problems such as data analysis, manufacturing and customer service. In that sense, AI is the umbrella term for technology that solves problems autonomously by simulating human intelligence.

They do this through algorithms designed to analyze input data and produce an output. Using chatbots, for example, the input data would be the customer's inquiry and the output would be the answer to the customer's inquiry held within the chatbot's database. What's more amazing is that AI algorithms can react to input data in various forms.

A great example is how you would ask a virtual assistant like Amazon Alexa or Google Assistant for the weather report. There are multiple ways that you can phrase that query, such as "what's the weather," "what's the temperature," or even "how's the weather looking." But by picking up on certain keywords, the virtual assistant can respond to all these different variants. "The current weather is 23 degrees Celsius with clear skies."

So, if that's the definition of AI, then how is machine learning different? Well, machine learning is generally considered to be a subset of AI. However, it's pretty common to hear the two terms used interchangeably. One major difference is that while AI still encompasses the idea of a sentient, conscious machine, machine learning as a term does not hold those connotations. AI is the grand vision of intelligent machines, while machine learning consists of the models, processes and supporting technology that we've been using to get there.

Diving a bit deeper now, within machine learning is another sector known as deep learning. In a nutshell, deep learning is machine learning at the largest scale, involving millions of instances of training data and often multiple layers within the neural network. As computing power and access to training data has increased, researchers have been able to develop machine learning models on a larger scale than ever before. Eventually, this led to the use of a new term, deep learning.

To fully understand its meaning, it's essential to first know a little bit about neural networks. Simply, a neural network is a collection of algorithms designed to take in data and find a solution with the least amount of error. They are called neural networks because the design of the nodes, inputs, and outputs slightly resemble that of a neuron in the human brain. The key differentiator between machine learning and deep learning is in the number of layers of nodes that the input data passes through. In the past, it was only possible to connect a few layers of nodes due to simple computing limitations. However, as technology has improved, it has become possible to build deeper neural networks with more hidden layers. This is the kind of model data scientists mean when they talk about deep learning.

To sum things up, AI is the grand all-encompassing vision of intelligent machines and machine learning is the processes and tools that are getting us there. Finally, deep learning is machine learning at the largest scale with more sophisticated neural networks, exponentially larger data sets and stronger computing power supporting it. With this in mind, it's possible to begin navigating through this complex exciting field and figuring out which processes will help to build out your own project.
