Lesson 21

Grammar - Ještě hlouběji do minulosti

Ještě než se pustíme do třetího typu podmínky, musíme se na chvíli zastavit a strojem času se dostat ještě hlouběji do minulosti, než sahají past simple a past continuous. Cože?! Ano, slyšíte dobře, angličtina umí vyjádřit i takzvanou předminulost.

Předminulý čas (past perfect) se používá k vyjádření dějů, které byly dokončeny ještě před nějakým jiným dějem v minulosti. Představte si, že potřebujete vyjádřit situaci, kdy jste například dokončili nějaký kus kódu na projektu a následně zjistili, že váš kolega tutéž část již udělal dříve, ale zapomněl ji pushnout na GitHub. Protože vyprávíte o svém programování v minulosti (I finished the task), ale kolega udělal svou činnost ještě před vámi, tedy hlouběji v minulosti, potřebujete právě předminulý čas (He had coded that part).

had + třetí minulý tvar slovesa

💎 Pro vyjádření akce dokončené před jinou minulou akcí

By the time the system crashed, the IT team had already backed up the data.

🔱 Pro ukázání příčiny a následku v minulosti

The server was down because the technician had unplugged it accidentally.

🙄 Pro vyjádření promeškané příležitosti v minulosti

They had not implemented the security patch and that's why the cyber attack went through.

💬 V nepřímé řeči

She said they had upgraded the software before the new policy was introduced.

⏭ Pro ukázání doby trvání akce až do jiného bodu v minulosti

The developer had worked on the project for three years before it was canceled.

💫 Pro vyjádření akce, která byla opakována až do určitého bodu v minulosti

The system had malfunctioned several times before they decided to replace it.

🙏 Ve výrazech přání o minulosti

She wished she had taken the cybersecurity course last year.

✨ Ve větách s třetím typem podmínky

If the programmer had tested the code thoroughly, he wouldn’t have faced so many bugs.

Dobré pochopení a správné použití past perfect může vaši komunikaci v IT prostředí zlepšit několika hned několika způsoby: Přesnější popis událostí: Ve světě IT je důležité přesně popisovat, kdy a v jakém pořadí došlo k určitým událostem, například při diagnostikování problémů nebo vysvětlování postupů. Minulý předčasný čas umožňuje zdůraznit, že jedna akce byla dokončena dříve než jiná minulá akce.

Efektivnější komunikace v týmu: V multikulturních a různorodých týmech, kde nemusí být angličtina prvním jazykem všech členů, může přesné použití gramatických past perfect zlepšit vzájemné porozumění a zabránit nedorozuměním ohledně načasování a posloupnosti událostí. Lepší analýza a řešení problémů: Při analýze problémů nebo incidentů v IT je důležité určit, které události se udály a v jakém pořadí. Past perfect pomáhá zřetelně odlišit akce, které se staly před jinými minulými událostmi, což je klíčové pro pochopení příčin a následků.

Vocabulary - Logical operators

Logické operátory jsou speciální symboly nebo slova používaná v programování a matematice k vykonávání logických operací. Tyto operátory jsou základním stavebním prvkem logických výrazů, které se používají k hodnocení pravdivosti nebo nepravdivosti určitých podmínek nebo výroků. Nejčastěji se používají v podmíněných výrazech, jako jsou if-else struktury, a v cyklech.

AND &&

Returns true if both operands are true.

🧩 If (age > 18 && age < 65) { eligible for employment. }

🧩 If (temperature > 0 && temperature < 100) { in liquid state. }

🧩 If (userIsLoggedIn && hasPrivileges) { access granted. }

🧩 Our office remains open if it's a business day and there are no public holidays.

🧩 You can access the advanced features of the software if you are a premium member and if your subscription is active.

🧩To qualify for the loan, you need to have a stable job and a good credit score.

OR ||

Returns true if at least one of the operands is true.

🧩 If (day == "Saturday" || day == "Sunday") { it's a weekend. }

🧩 If (temperature > 30 || humidity > 80%) { turn on air conditioning. }

🧩 If (isAdmin || isModerator) { access to the dashboard. }

🧩 You can save the file as a PDF or a Word document.

🧩 She will buy a new phone or a laptop with her bonus.

🧩 We will go to the beach or have a picnic this weekend.

NOT !

Returns true if the operand is false, and vice versa.

🧩 If (!userIsLoggedIn) { redirect to login page. }

🧩 If (!validInput) { display error message. }

🧩 While (!fileEndReached) { read next line. }

🧩 If the user is not an administrator, they cannot access the system settings.

🧩 We need to update the software on all computers that are not currently running the latest version.

🧩 You can edit the document only if you are not in view-only mode.

Listening - History of laptops

When you think of a laptop, you most likely have something like this in mind, but find out that the first laptops weighed dozens of kilograms and were not really portable. Let's find the history of laptops and how they have evolved over time.

The foundations of the evolution of portable computers were laid in 1975, when IBM launched the first portable computer, the IBM 5100. You couldn't hold it in your lap, because it was very heavy, weighing 25kg. However, it was portable and came with a 5-inch screen, a 1.9 MHz processor, and used a magnetic tape cartridge for data storage.

But in 1981 Osborne 1 was launched, considered by many to be the first truly portable laptop. It weighed 11 kg and could be closed and transported like a suitcase. The Osborne 1 had a 5-inch screen and was the first computer to have 2 built-in floppy disk drives, and was powered by a 4MHz processor and 64KB of RAM. The Osborne 1 was a real success, both from the point of view of portability and the rather low price, only 6,000 USD, in today's price.

Immediately, the Grid Compass was launched, which by its design set a standard for modern laptops. The Grid Compass 1101 was the first clamshell laptop, and due to its small size, it was used by NASA in their space shuttle program at the time. The laptop was equipped with an Intel 8086 processor and had a 320 x 240 pixel display, all in a package that weighed 5kg.

Epson launched in 1982, the first portable computer with a built-in printer, which was small enough to fit in a briefcase. The Epson HX-20 featured a 68-key keyboard, rechargeable batteries, a very small LCD screen (120×32 pixels) and 16 KB of RAM, all for the price of just $759, about $2,300 today.

Built in 1985, the first device with a modern form factor for a laptop was launched. The aluminum Kaypro 2000 came with a removable keyboard, LCD screen and lead-acid battery. All for $1995, about $5,600 today. In 1986, IMB launched the Convertible PC, with a rather intriguing design and shape. It had a removable 640 x 200 LCD screen, a 3.5 inch floppy drive and weighed 6kg. The laptop cost about $5,600 in today's money, but it was not at all a success, as a new laptop came on the market that promised to "offer twice the computing power of the IBM laptop for less money." This is the Toshiba T1100. It had more RAM, a 640 x 200 pixel monochrome LCD display and was powered by the 7.16 MHz Intel 80C86 processor.

In 1987, Hewlett-Packard distinguished itself with the Vectra Portable CS, the first laptop with a 3-inch floppy drive capable of using 1.44 MB floppy disks. However, this was not enough, and the Vectra Portable was not successful, both because of its large size and its high price.

In 1989, Apple entered the laptop market with the Macintosh Portable, Apple's first battery-powered computer. It had a 9.8-inch black-and-white screen, a 40 MB hard drive, trackball and weighed 7.2 kg. Moreover, this is considered the worst Apple product ever made, if the computer's battery failed, it could no longer work, and the small storage space and off-white color and high price led to the failure of the Macintosh Portable. Apple learned from its mistakes, and introduced the PowerBook series in 1991, which initiated the form factor standards for laptops. Clasp closure, screen over the keyboard, larger screens, support for hands and trackpad.

The following year, IBM launched the ThinkPad 700C, considered an important stone in the evolution of laptops, being the best-known, best-selling and most appreciated IBM laptop. The ThinkPad came with a 10.4-inch color LCD display, was powered by the Intel 486SLC 25 MHz processor, had 4 MB of RAM, and had an 80 or 120 MB hard drive. The laptop weighed only 2.6 kg and was sold starting at 2375 USD, which is 5000 USD today.

Apple's PowerBook series initially featured optional color displays on the PowerBook 165c model, and with the 1994 500 series, Apple introduced the first true touchpad, the first 16-bit stereo audio, and the first built-in Ethernet network adapter. The price for such a model was about $2,270, which is $4,600 today.

Also in 1944, IMB introduced the first laptop with an integrated CD-ROM drive, the ThinkPad 775CD model. Now that the form factor for a laptop has been established, the big manufacturers are trying to innovate on the design and size side. Apple stood out with the iBook series in 1999, which came with an unusual design inspired by the iMac design of the time. However, the iBook stood out by introducing Wi-fi connectivity, being the first with such a feature.

Faster than we expected, in 2002 the first gaming laptop was launched, the Alienware Area-51M model. It wasn't cheap at all, but for $3000, the laptop offered top specs at the time. A 3.06GHz Pentium 4 processor, 1GB of 266MHz memory, 40GB of storage and a 2048 x 1536 UXGA screen. Also in the 2000s, Dell was the leader in the laptop market, being very popular and cool for a long time. It was also then that Toshiba launched the world's thinnest laptop, the Toshiba Portege 2000. It had a front edge of only 1.49 cm high, extending slightly to 1.91 cm at the back.

During this whole period, we tried to make the laptops as small, thin and performant at the same time. But the one that stood out was Apple with the MacBook Air from 2008. This was the thinnest laptop in the world measuring just 0.4cm at its thinnest point and 1.9cm at its thickest point. At the same time, it was the first laptop without an integrated optical drive. In 2012, Microsoft launched the Microsoft Surface, being the first to successfully combine tablet and laptop. However, this type of devices were not very successful, many considering that the two devices should remain separate.

If at the beginning of the 80s there was only the idea of a portable computer, so that later we had what we now call "laptops", today we have a choice of many categories of laptops, depending on everyone's needs and budget, whether you want a laptop thin, with high autonomy, thin frames, powerful or convertible hardware.
