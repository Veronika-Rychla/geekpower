Listening - Biografie Steva Jobse

In August 2008, Apple became the first trillion-dollar US company in history. Welcome to our animated show of history. This episode presents the history of the world's first trillion-dollar company. Apple was founded by Steve Jobs and Steve Wozniak in April 1976. Steven Paul Jobs was born to Abdul Fattah John Dalli and Joanne Siebel in San Francisco but was adopted by Paul Jobs and Claire Hagopian, a Bay Area blue-collar couple whom Jobs was extremely grateful to and always considered as his parents.

By the time he was a teen, Jobs was deeply involved in electronics and befriended many of the engineers in the neighborhood. While studying at Monta Loma Elementary School in Mountain View, Jobs hardly made any friends and was frequently bullied, considering dropping out of school.

Jobs was given a summer job by Bill Hewlett. He then entered Homestead High School with Bill Fernandes, a fellow electronics hobbyist. They decided to enroll in John McCollum's electronics class, but the rebellious Jobs would eventually clash with McCollum, losing interest in the class. Jobs and Steve Wozniak were introduced in 1971 by a mutual friend. Thanks to their shared love of technology, the two Steves attended the Homebrew Computer Club, a computer hobbyist group in California's Menlo Park.

Woz produced the first computer with a typewriter-like keyboard and the ability to connect to a regular TV as a screen. This computer was later named the Apple One, which was the archetype of every modern computer. Recognizing its brilliance, Jobs sold his VW Microbus to help fund its production. Wozniak sold his HP calculator. Together, they founded Apple Computer Inc. in April 1976, alongside Ronald Wayne. Jobs convinced him to take 10% of the company stock and act as an arbiter should he and Woz come to blows. However, Wayne backed out soon after, selling his share for just $500, a holding that would've been worth seventy-two billion dollars decades later. Apple's very first logo was drawn by Ronald Wayne, depicting Isaac Newton under an apple tree. It was then redesigned with a rainbow scheme and was used for over two decades until Steve Jobs updated the Apple logo after returning from an apple farm.

A year after its foundation, the Apple 2 was released. It was a more commercial product, equipped with a processor running at just one megahertz and four kilobytes of RAM. It became one of the three "1977 Trinity" computers, generally credited with creating the home computer market. The Apple 3, designed to take on the business environment, faced challenges, leading the company to recall thousands of units.

In 1980, Apple launched the IPO of its stock, instantly creating more millionaires than any company in history. The early Apple computers were text-based machines, much like the earliest IBM PCs. But Jobs envisioned something more intuitive, leading to the launch of the Lisa in 1983 with its graphical user interface. The Macintosh debuted the following year. After a long power struggle, Jobs was forced out of Apple in 1985. He founded NeXT, specializing in computers for higher education and business markets. Apple purchased NeXT in 1996, which brought Jobs back and provided the foundation for the Mac OS X operating system.

In 1997, Jobs stepped in as the CEO to begin a critical restructuring of the company. At a Macworld Expo, he announced a partnership with Microsoft. This included a commitment from Microsoft to release Microsoft Office for Macintosh and a $150 million US dollar investment in Apple. One of Jobs' first moves was to develop the iMac, an all-in-one Macintosh desktop computer, released in 1998. Apple had suffered setbacks as consumers increasingly opted for Wintel machines over Apple's models. The iPod was then released in 2001. Three years later, the iTunes Music Store was launched, and in 2007, the first version of the iPhone became publicly available. The MacBook Air, released in 2008, was a significant change in Apple's laptop line, influencing a whole new category of computers called ultrabooks. In August 2011, Jobs passed away due to complications related to a pancreatic tumor. Apple subsequently acquired Beats in 2014 and released the Apple Watch a year later.

The iPhone has become one of the most widely used smartphones globally, helping Apple become one of the world's most valuable publicly traded companies. Are you using an iPhone?
