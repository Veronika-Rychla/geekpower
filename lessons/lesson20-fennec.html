Lesson 20

Grammar - Wishful thinking

Jak už bylo řečeno v předchozí lekci, reálné podmínky (Zero Conditional a First Conditional) se používají k vyjádření skutečných možností v budoucnosti. Jde o situace, které se opravdu mohou stát v současnosti nebo v budoucnu, a jsou považovány za pravděpodobné nebo možné.

🧩 If it rains tomorrow, we will cancel the picnic. = opravdu tento scénář může nastat, jde tedy o podmínku zcela reálnou

🧩 If you click 'archive,' the document disappeaers, but it's not deleted. = takto se aplikace chová, kdykoli uživatel klikne na tlačítko Uložit

Second a Third Conditional jsou však takzvané nereálné podmínky. Vyjadřují situace hypotetické nebo takové, které jsou nepravděpodobné či zcela imaginární. Pojďme se nyní podívat na Second Conditional.

💡 Wishful thinking 💡

"Wishful thinking" je termín používaný k popisu situace, kdy si člověk přeje nebo doufá, že se něco stane, i když je to nepravděpodobné nebo nereálné. Jde o druh myšlení, který je více založen na přáních a nadějích než na logice nebo skutečnosti. Wishful thinking často ignoruje realitu ve prospěch optimistického nebo idealizovaného výsledku.

🧩 Thinking that buying a lottery ticket will ensure retirement is pure wishful thinking.

🧩 Hoping that simply wanting a new job will result in job offers without applying is a classic example of wishful thinking.

🧩 Believing that a complex software bug will fix itself overnight is wishful thinking.

🧩 Believing the team can finish the project in one week, despite needing two weeks for similar projects in the past, is wishful thinking.

🎐 Second Conditional aneb wishful thinking

If + past simple, would + základní tvar slovesa

Second Conditional se používá k vyjádření hypotetických nebo nepravděpodobných situacích a jejich možných výsledcích. Je zvláště užitečný pro představování scénářů v současnosti nebo budoucnosti, které nejsou reálné, ale jsou zajímavé na přemýšlení nebo diskusi.

Část věty s 'if' používá prostý minulý čas. Přestože se používá minulý čas, význam není o minulosti; jedná se o hypotetickou nebo nereálnou situaci v současnosti nebo budoucnosti. Hlavní část věty (výsledek 'if') používá 'would' následované základním tvarem slovesa.

Hypothetical situations that are currently not true or are imagined.

🧩 If I learned Java, I would develop my own mobile app. = v reálu ale Javu neumím, typické wishful thinking

🧩 If she were a software engineer, she would understand that code. = ale je UX designérka, proto kódu nerozumí

🧩 If I were an expert in artificial intelligence, I would build a smart assistant. = typické wishful thinking

Unlikely scenarios that are possible but not very likely to happen.

🧩 If our company bought more servers, we would increase our data storage capacity. = nicméně víme, že management škudlí a nepustí fondy

🧩 If this computer were more powerful, it would run advanced simulations. = ale nemáme na lepší počítač peníze

🧩 If he had a faster internet connection, he would download the files much quicker. = ale tatínek o rychlejší internet nestojí, radši si zajde mezitím udělat kafe 😂

To give advice or suggest actions in hypothetical situations.

🧩 If we used cloud computing, we would reduce our hardware costs. = navrhujeme, co by se mohlo teoreticky udělat

🧩 If our website had a better design, it would attract more visitors. = návrh, že by teoreticky mohlo pomoct vylepšit vzhled webu

🧩 If we implemented better security protocols, we would protect our data from hackers. = návrh, co by teoreticky mohlo pomoci

🤔 K čemu je to dobré v IT?

V IT prostředí je Second Conditional zvláště užitečný pro diskusi o hypotetických scénářích, potenciálních řešeních a plánování budoucích možností. Díky své struktuře pro představování situací, které v současnosti nejsou pravdivé, ale mohly by být za jiných okolností, slouží různým účelům:

Spekulace o o potenciálním dopadu nových technologií

🧩 If we integrated this new software, it would improve our workflow.

Zvažování hypotetických řešení technických problémů.

🧩 If we increased the server's bandwidth, we would reduce website downtime.

Plánování Zdrojů

🧩 If we had a larger development team, we could develop more features simultaneously.

Projektový management

🧩 If the client changed their requirements, we would need to adjust our timeline.

Osobní rozvoj

🧩 If you learned Python, you could automate many of our routine tasks.

Inovace

🧩 If we developed a new algorithm, we could solve this issue more efficiently.

Second Conditional je v IT užitečný pro jeho schopnost podporovat myšlení do budoucna, umožňující profesionálům vizualizovat a plánovat různé potenciální budoucnosti v rychle se vyvíjejícím oboru.

Vocabulary - Matematické operátory

Addition /ˈædɪʃən/ (+) /plʌs/ = sčítání, plus

Adds two values. Example: a + b.

🧩 To calculate the total price, we add the item price and tax together using "total = price + tax."

🧩 We add several numbers to get the total.

🧩 Computers add numbers very fast.

Subtraction /səbˈtrækʃən/ (-) /maɪnəs/ = odečítání, mínus

Subtracts one value from another. Example: a - b.

🧩 To find the remaining balance, subtract the amount spent from the initial balance using "balance = initialBalance - amountSpent."

🧩 If we subtract 5 from 10, we get 5.

🧩 To find out how much space is left, we subtract the used space from the total.

Multiplication /ˌmʌltɪplɪˈkeɪʃən/ (*) /taɪmz/ = násobení, krát

Multiplies two values. Example: a * b.

🧩 To get the area of a rectangle, multiply its length by its width using "area = length * width."

🧩 Multiplying by zero always gives zero.

🧩 Multiplying points by 2 doubles the score.

Division /dɪˈvɪʒən/ (/) /dɪˈvaɪdɪd baɪ/ = dělení, děleno (čím)

Divides one value by another. Example: a / b.

🧩 To calculate the average score, divide the sum of all scores by the number of scores using "average = sumOfScores / numberOfScores."

🧩 We use division to share things equally.

🧩 Dividing by one gives the same number.

Modulus /ˈmɒdjʊləs/ (%) /pɜːˈsɛnt/ = modulo

Modulus tells us what remains after division. Example: a % b.

🧩 To find out if a number is even or odd, use the modulus operator as in "isEven = (number % 2) == 0."

🧩 Modulus is like finding the leftover piece.

🧩 We use modulus to find out if a number is even or odd.

Exponentiation /eksˈpəʊnənʃɪˈeɪʃən/ (** to the power of) /tʊ ðə paʊər ɒv/

Raises one value to the power of another. Example: a ** b.

🧩 To calculate the power of a number, use the exponentiation operator like "result = base ** exponent in Python."

🧩 In math, exponentiation is like multiplying a number by itself.

🧩 Exponentiation can show how things grow fast.

Listening - If/else statements

Let's talk about conditional statements. Conditional statements let us perform actions based on if a condition is met. We use them to help direct the behavior of our programs. In fact, we actually use conditional statements in our everyday lives. Have you ever gone to a cafe but didn't really know what you wanted to order? You've probably narrowed down what you want, kind of like this: "Do I want something refreshing or something else? If I want something refreshing, I guess I could get something like an iced coffee or an iced tea. I guess I'll get some tea, but what flavor do I want? Oh god, there's so many flavors to choose from. Am I feeling a little adventurous, or should I pick something safe? Something tart, maybe something citrusy, maybe strawberry. But can I even afford this? What am I doing with my life? Do my parents even love me? Did I leave the oven on?"

Well, to help our indecisive mind figure out what we want, we can use something called a flowchart. Flowcharts help us plan and visualize the different outcomes in our program. Looking back at our cafe scenario, we can use a flowchart to figure out what we want to get. First, we ask ourselves, "Do we want something refreshing?" If not, "Do we want something hot?" If we do, then we'll get a smoothie, but if not, we'll just not get anything at all and go home. But if we choose to get a refreshing drink, then we ask ourselves, "Do you want tea?" If not, then we'll get an iced coffee. But if I do want tea, then do I want raspberry? If we do, then great, we'll get raspberry, but if not, we'll just get some kiwi-flavored tea instead.

In programming, you can represent these decisions with what's known as an if statement. An if statement is a conditional statement where if the condition is met, then an action happens. So, if I'm in the mood for something refreshing, then I'll order a fruity drink. Now that we have an idea of what an if statement is, let's see how we go about writing one.

As a disclaimer, even though writing an if statement is pretty much the same in most modern languages, it's important to check your specific language to make sure you didn't miss anything. Anyways, back to the video. We start an if statement by simply putting the word "if" at the beginning of two parentheses. Then you would follow up with a condition. A condition is something that we check to see if it's true, and if it is, the computer will execute whatever's inside our little curly brackets. Like, is three greater than two?
